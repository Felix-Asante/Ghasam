GHASAM E-Care

Terms & Condition

  • The main purpose and existence of the GHASAM “Emergency/Early/Easy”-Care (GHASAM E-Care) is to serve as a welfare fund to attend to the non-academic needs of the Ghanaian student in Morocco in times of need and difficulty. The packages of the E-Care are three-pronged:
    • Health Insurance
    • Support for Bereaved Students
    • Financial aid in the form of loans
  • The packages and services of the E-Care place priority on students that have subscribed to the Ghasam E-Care even though the goal is to make services and packages available to all students. Nonetheless, the following packages can be accessed by non-subscribers of the E-Care.
    • Emergency Medical situations (financial support).
    • Assistance in filling and presentation of AXA medical documents.
    • Calling for appeal for funds
    • Assistance in procuring flight tickets from secretariat for bereaved students.
  • Accessibility to services and packages of Ghasam E-Care to non-subscribers will be decided by the Ghasam E-Care committee based on the amount of funds in the Ghasam welfare coffers, number of subscribers and amount of sponsorship per year.
  • Demand for anonymous to the student body, during financial accounts and Ghasam posts, is possible for the following packages.
    • Financial aid(Loans)
    • Sponsors
  • Demand for anonymous is not possible during financial accounts to Auditor and NSC.
  • Subscribers who have never benefited from the GHASAM E-Care at the end of every 3 years shall be appreciated with customised GHASAM gifts.

  • The welfare fund shall provide services in assisting subscribers in their demand for AMCI health insurance and make follow ups on their behalf when necessary.
  • The welfare fund shall cater for the rest of medical expenses (20%) not covered by the AMCI insurance and transportation costs up to 100dhs for emergency situations.
  • Financial payment for all medical expenses of the Ghasam welfare fund shall be made after payment of AMCI health insurance confirmed by a picture of payment slip to Ghasam E-care committee or Ghasam President.
  • Payment of medical expenses by the Ghasam welfare fund before payment by AMCI Insurance is possible only for Emergency Medical situations.
  • The Amount of money to release to student before payment by AMCI Insurance shall be decided by the Ghasam E-care Committee with terms and conditions with a minimum amount of 30% of total medical cost.
  • Condition of assistance for expensive emergency medical procedures (Above 10 000dhs) shall be evaluated by the Ghasam E-care committee and based on the capacity of the E-care coffers at that moment, an appeal for funds may be raised to support the remaining amount after initial payment from the E-care.
  • Money paid for emergency conditions before refund from AMCI or Secretariat shall be regarded as a Ghasam Loan without interest. Students are expected to pay back the 80-90% of the loan without interest to the E-care coffers once AMCI or Secretariat refunds.

  • Family Relatives eligible for the Welfare Support Package shall be Parents and guardians of Ghanaian students in Morocco.
  • Financial support and assistance in securing plane tickets are exclusive to the unfortunate demise of a Parent or legal guardian of Ghanaian student in Morocco.
  • Gift vouchers and hampers shall be made available to students in the unfortunate loss of a sibling.
  • Amount Allocated per student in case of bereavement shall be ultimately decided by the E-care committee with reference to the finance section of the Ghasam E-Care terms and conditions
  • One of the following documents is needed in order to benefit from the Welfare support package.
    • Medical Death Certificate
    • Funeral Posters
    • Morgue receipts

  • Financial aid in times of difficulties shall be made available to Ghanaian students in the form of loans called GHASAM Loans
  • Financial Crisis shall be defined as non-payment of Embassy stipends for 60 or more consecutive days.
  • All Ghasam loans have a refund duration of 3 months. Failure to refund loan shall be subjected to a penalty which will be decided by the E-care committee and the National Executive Committee.
  • Issuing of Ghasam loans to students during financial crises shall be at a rate of 5% for subscribers and 10% to non-subscribers for a refund duration of 3 months . Hence, the interest is counted for every 3 months. (Dependent on the financial capacity of the E-Care coffers)
  • Issuing of Ghasam loans to students in the absence of financial crises shall be at a rate of 7% for subscribers and 14% for non-subscribers for a refund duration of 3 months. (Dependent on the financial capacity of the E-Care coffers)
  • The amount limit of funds allocated to students and approval for Ghasam loans shall be decided by the Ghasam E-care committee with reference to the finance section of Ghasam E-care terms and condition.
  • Ghasam E-care Subscribers shall be prioritized over non-subscribers in the issuing of Ghasam loans.
  • Beneficiaries of Ghasam loans must agree to GHASAM E-Care terms and conditions before release of funds.
  • Ghasam loan demand form. (See Annex)
  • Target number of student subscription to Ghasam E-Care shall be One hundred and above (100+) per year.
  • Students in good standing with the Embassy of Ghana and/or AMCI shall be eligible to subscribe to the Ghasam E-Care.
  • Subscription fee for the Ghasam E-Care is set at an amount of 200 MAD annually. Subscription fee can be changed only by majority vote of the general assembly.
  • Subscription fee shall be paid to city leaders in the form of quarterly (50 MAD), semi-annually (100 MAD) or full payment (200 MAD) of the annual subscription fee.
    • NB: Future proposal for annual deduction of subscription fee at the embassy (when approved by the student body.)
  • Subscription form is downloadable here (Also see Annex)
  • Completed forms should be submitted to the Ville leader, to be forwarded to the E-Care Committee.
  • The Ghasam E-Care Committee shall be made of 3 members in good standing with the student body and the Ghasam association, with the financial secretary a default member of the committee.
  • The criteria for selection of committee members shall be based on belief and dedication to the Ghasam E-care project, integrity and popularity of the applicant among Ghanaian students, professionalism and ability to work in a team and with people.
  • Selection process of E-Care Committee Chairman and Members shall be proposed by outgoing E-care Committee members and approved by Ghasam NSC. In the absence or difficulty of approval by the NSC, The Electoral Commissioner shall conduct general assembly elections and approval of the applicant shall be made by majority vote of the general assembly.
  • The selection process shall be made before handing over of NEC executives and the newly approved committee shall assume full responsibility after the swearing in of a new Ghasam NEC administration.
  • The NSC can propose applicants for Ghasam E-care Committee for approval by the NEC and the current E-care committee members. In the absence or difficulty of approval by the NEC and committee members, The Electoral Commissioner shall conduct general assembly elections and approval of the applicant shall be made by majority vote of the general assembly.
  • Members of E-care committee shall work solely with Ghasam NEC and city leaders in attending to the welfare needs of the Ghanaian student.
  • The main work of the committee shall be deliberation on allocation of funds for packages of Ghasam E-care project to students and assisting and follow up on AMCI health insurance and filling of forms. Decisions are made by majority vote of the committee.
  • The NEC shall be responsible for following up on procuring flight tickets for bereaved students.
  • There shall be allocation of Funds for Ghasam E-care committee at the beginning of the academic year. The amount will be decided by the NSC and should be a minimum of 1 000 MAD (or an amount deemed necessary by the NSC)
  • The E-care committee shall be subjected to audit at the end of every quarter within the academic year.
  • GHASAM E-Care funds shall be managed in the Association bank account.
  • In the absence of the Association bank Account, the E-care funds shall be managed in a separate bank account created by the Financial Secretary and supervised by the GHASAM President, as well as the E-Care Committee Chairman.
  • Quarterly accounts by the committee to the NSC and the student body within the Academic year.

    Source of funding for the Ghasam E-care project shall be from:

    • Annual Subscription fees
    • Partners (Ghana Scholarship secretariat, Ghana Embassy, GHAMOSA)
    • Sponsors
    • Investments (in the future)
  • Financial Plan for the E-Care Project
    • Less than 15 000dhs in E-Care coffers: Packages available are only assistance and follow ups for Health insurance and acquisition of plane ticket for bereaved students
    • 15 000dhs-35 000dhs in E-Care coffers: Packages Offered are Health insurance and financial aid in time of crisis (800dhs-1 500dhs per student per annum), support for bereaved students (1 000dhs- 2 000dhs per student per annum).
    • 35 000dhs-50 000dhs in E-care coffers: Packages Offered are Health insurance and financial aid in times of crisis (1 500dhs-2 500dhs per student per annum), support for bereaved students (2 000dhs-3 000dhs per student per annum).
    • 50 000dhs-100 000dhs in E-care coffers: 3 000dhs-5 000dhs per student per annum for both Health insurance, financial aid in times of crisis and support for bereaved students.
    • Plus 100 000dhs in Ghasam E-care coffers: : 5 000dhs-8 000dhs per student per annum for both Health insurance, financial aid in times of crisis and support for bereaved students. Financial Aid for students
    • Plus 200 000dhs in Ghasam E-Care coffers: No limit for funds per student.
    • Basic Limit Amount in the Ghasam coffers at all times: 10 000dhs
  • Financial Goal of the Ghasam E-Care Project
    • 50 000dhs after the first year of its inauguration.
    • 100 000dhs after the second year of its installation.
    • +150 000dhs from the third year of its installation.
  • The intentional presentation of false information or documents is subjected to non-payment of E-Care support.
  • Penalties for mismanagement of funds, delay in the payment of Ghasam loans or obstruction of duty by student or leader towards the Ghasam E-care committee shall be deliberated by the committee and the NSC when deemed necessary.
